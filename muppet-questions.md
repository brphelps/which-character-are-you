Unified Henson Character Inventory (UHCI) – Question Set

Response scale for all questions:
1 = Strongly Disagree · 10 = Strongly Agree

Event Drive (ED) – Initiation & Agency (5 questions)

Q1. I usually initiate action rather than wait for others to decide what happens.
→ ED

Q2. When a plan is unclear, I step in to define next steps.
→ ED

Q3. I am comfortable steering group outcomes, even if others resist.
→ ED + SA (agency in social context)

Q4. If nothing is happening, I feel compelled to make something happen.
→ ED

Q5. I prefer being responsible for outcomes rather than reacting to them.
→ ED

Emotional Containment (EC) – Regulation & Impulse Control (5 questions)

Q6. My emotional reactions are noticeable and hard to hide. (reverse-scored)
→ EC

Q7. I can stay composed even when things go wrong.
→ EC

Q8. Strong emotions tend to override my better judgment. (reverse-scored)
→ EC

Q9. Others often rely on me to remain calm in chaotic situations.
→ EC

Q10. I act on impulse before thinking things through. (reverse-scored)
→ EC

Social Aim (SA) – Communal vs Self-Directed Orientation (5 questions)

Q11. I prioritize group wellbeing over my personal preferences.
→ SA

Q12. Helping others succeed is more important to me than being recognized.
→ SA

Q13. I enjoy cooperation even when it slows me down.
→ SA

Q14. I’m comfortable acting against group norms if it suits me. (reverse-scored)
→ SA

Q15. I feel responsible for the emotional state of those around me.
→ SA + EC

Reality Lens (RL) – Literal ↔ Absurd Cognitive Style (5 questions)

Q16. I prefer clear rules and concrete explanations over playful ambiguity. (reverse-scored)
→ RL

Q17. I enjoy nonsense, exaggeration, or illogical humor.
→ RL

Q18. I often interpret situations in a literal, straightforward way. (reverse-scored)
→ RL

Q19. Absurdity feels like a valid way to explore truth.
→ RL

Q20. I am comfortable when logic bends or contradicts itself.
→ RL

Show-Awareness (SH) – Meta & Performative Awareness (5 questions)

Q21. I am aware of how I appear to an audience, even in serious moments.
→ SH

Q22. I sometimes exaggerate my behavior for effect.
→ SH

Q23. I act as though I’m “in the scene,” not observing it from outside. (reverse-scored)
→ SH

Q24. I knowingly play a role that others expect from me.
→ SH + BS

Q25. I’m comfortable breaking immersion for a joke or commentary.
→ SH

Behavioral Stability (BS) – Consistency Across Contexts (5 questions)

Q26. My personality stays consistent across different situations.
→ BS

Q27. People generally know what to expect from me.
→ BS

Q28. My reactions change dramatically depending on my mood. (reverse-scored)
→ BS

Q29. Stress causes me to behave very differently than usual. (reverse-scored)
→ BS + EC

Q30. I maintain the same values and behavior even under pressure.
→ BS
